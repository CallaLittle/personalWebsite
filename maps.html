Maps

East Coast Cycle Tour

 

This map was made for my Cartography and Graphic Design class. It depicts a bike race course across the Appalachians. The main goal of this assignment was to create a nice relief map. A detailed representation of the area was made in ArcMap and then combined with a generalized version made in Terrain Sculptor. The rest of the rest of the work was done in Photoshop and Illustrator. This map was one of my favorites to create.

Scarred Earth

 

I created this map as a final project in my introductory cartography class. The topic was open to whatever we wished to map. Since I am interested in physical geography and earth sciences, I chose something that would fit my interests. The map depicts locations of impact craters in North America. I chose to map North America because it had the most data available. I used graduated symbols to represent the size of the meteor impact and applied a sequential color scheme to represent their age. This map won second place in the Barbara Bartz Petchenik Memorial Award in Cartographic Design.

Mario Kart 64

 

This was my first attempt at creating a custom tileset in mapbox. I chose to mimic the artistic style of Mario Kart 64, which was one of my favorite games when I was growing up. This tileset was a challenge to make. I included many textures and spent a lot of time in photoshop learning how to make them render seemlessly. It was also my first time using CartoCSS, which took a little bit to adjust to. I think one of the most frustrating things was having to apply the same style to every location if it was in the same class. It resulted in the map looking good in some places and poor in others. I look forward to spending more time creating interactive maps. You can see the full tile set here.

Sailing the Great Lakes

 

This map was created for my dad, who was a sailor on the Great Lakes before I was born. He used to tell my brother and me stories about his time on the boats, so I wanted to create a map for him to remember his time there. My goal was to create something "pretty" and steer away from making it a straight reference map, but I did want to include pieces from the nautical charts he used to use so I included navigation headings and a nautical compass rose.

Hanoi Landcover Classification

 

This map was an attempt to make an attractive representation of landcover classification for Hanoi, Vietnam. I am interested in working with creating visually appealing remote sensing results. Due to the large number of classes in many classifications, it becomes difficult to display them attractively. Due to the fact that I love remote sensing and cartography, this is something I plan to explore more in the future.
